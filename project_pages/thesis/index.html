My M.S. thesis at the University of Wisconsin–Madison was a visual storytelling design study intended to address the persistent gap between critical geographic scholarship and conventional cartographic representation--specifically, the failure of cartographers to ethically and effectively represent the structural and historical forces driving Guatemala–US migration, as well as the personal and emotional repercussions of displacement. To address this problem, I carried out a hybrid design process that synthesized visual storytelling practices from critical cartography and data journalism in an attempt to balance ethics and usability.
